Modern Dystopia

Curated by: Joseph DeFilippo

In the modern age of the internet, privacy is a thing of the past. Every button we click, message we send, every location we visit, even the facial expressions we make in some cases, are all tracked. The internet and its large corporations know more about us than we do ourselves, and the three net artworks Iâ€™ve gathered for this exhibition are in response to this ever growing lack of privacy. Each of them calls to attention just how much of our personal information is essentially open to the public, and how easily it is tracked and traded between corporations, or even between individuals. All three pieces take an aspect of our personal data and manipulate it to call to attention how little of our own personal information we still control.
